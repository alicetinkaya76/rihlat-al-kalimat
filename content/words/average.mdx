      en: |
        From the thirteenth century onwards, the merchant-consulates of the Mediterranean port-cities like Venice, Genoa, Marseille and Barcelona were in intense commercial contact with the Arab merchant-centres of Alexandria-Damascus-Tripoli. The Mediterranean merchant-lingua franca (the Arab-Italian-Provençal hybrid working-language) inherited the Arab maritime-law term *ʿawārīya* in the Italian form *avaria* / Provençal *avaria*. Its first Latin written record is in the Marseille-consulate records of 1268; the 1340 Venetian *Capitulare Nauticum* (maritime-code) adopted the word as a legal standard.

        The development-history of European medieval maritime-law: in the fourteenth century, the *Consolato del Mare* (the Mediterranean sea-consulate law, of Catalan-Aragonese origin) and England's *Black Book of the Admiralty* (around 1336), independently but with parallel themes, incorporated the Arab *ʿawārīya* principle into the Christian-Latin maritime-law standards. The modern forms of the word — French *avarie* (maritime) and English *average* (general-mean) — divided semantically in the sixteenth-seventeenth century; the same word began to live in two distinct practical-worlds.

        The English semantic-extension: in the seventeenth-century maritime-law, *general average* meant "the damage shared by all the traders in common"; the mathematical use emerged in the early eighteenth century, shifting from *general* to *mean / typical* sense. The concept of common-sharing (1/n per trader) → abstracted as the mathematical "the sum of n elements divided by n". This semantic shift is a case-instance of *concept-borrowing-via-metaphor* in modern comparative-lexicology.

      en: |
        The technical-legal infrastructure of the ninth-twelfth-century Mediterranean maritime-trade of the Islamic-Arab world possessed written standards like *Kitāb akriyat al-sufun* (The Book of Ship-Hire, Egypt, ninth century) and *Akhbār al-ʿawārīya* (Records of Damage-Cases). *Al-Mudawwana al-Kubrā* (the basic legal book of the Maliki fiqh-school, ninth century) had systematically standardised the principles of the cooperative-damage-sharing of Mediterranean sea-voyage. The principle of *ʿawārīya* (the proportional-sharing of the sea-sacrifice-loss among all the loaders) became the fundamental legal-principle of the sea-trade of the Islamic world.

        Practical mechanism: when a Mediterranean merchant-ship set off, each trader's cargo was loaded under a separate contract; on the sea-voyage, in emergencies like storm-sinking-pirate-attack, the ship-captain could be forced to throw some cargo into the sea or damage it (since the intervention prevented the loss of the entire ship). After the voyage, in port, an Arab *ḥakam* (maritime-arbiter) and witness-traders calculated the distribution of the loss: they distributed the value of the lost cargo in proportion to the total-value of all the cargo on board. The written standards of this practical-principle are abundantly documented in eleventh-century Andalusi texts of *ʿAbd al-Karīm ibn ʿAlī al-Khūshanī* and in the *Geniza* documents (the eleventh-century Fustat-Cairo Jewish merchant archive).

        Modern comparative-legal literature (S. D. Goitein's *A Mediterranean Society*, 1967-93; Olivia Constable's *Trade and Traders in Muslim Spain*, 2003) proves that Islamic-Mediterranean maritime-law is the direct-foundation of modern European maritime-insurance; the word *ʿawārīya* is the living linguistic-signature of this legal-transfer.

      tr: |
        Pre-İslam Arabistan'da kervan-tüccar (*qāfila*) ve deniz-tüccar (*tājir al-baḥr*) ortakları yolculuk-zararının paylaşımı pratiğini sözlü hukuk-geleneğinden devralmıştı. *ʿawārīya* kökü Sami fiil kökü *ʿ-w-r* "hasar-görme, kusur-bulma" — Akkadca *ḥalāqu* (yıkım), İbranice *ʿāwâ* (eğri-olmak, hatalı-olmak) ile akraba. Mekke-Medine ticaret kervanlarının (Kureyş tüccarları, 6.-7. yy) yıllık iki-mevsim kervan-organizasyonunda ortak-zarar paylaşımı standart-prensipti; Kuran'da "kış ve yaz kervan-yolculuğu" (Kuran 106:2) ayetinin altında bu pratik-ekonomik altyapı yatar.

        7. yy İslam fethi sonrası, pre-İslam Arap kervan-tüccarlık pratiği Akdeniz ve Hint Okyanusu deniz-ticaret pratiğine entegre edildi. Şam (636 fethi) ve İskenderiye (642 fethi) eski-Bizans tüccar-merkezleri Arap-İslam dünyasına geçtiğinde, Bizans denizci-hukuk geleneği *Lex Rhodia* (Eski-Yunan Rodos-deniz-hukuk, MS Ö 3. yy itibariyle Akdeniz standardı) ile Arap pre-İslam *ʿawārīya* pratiği sentez edildi. Bu sentez 8.-9. yy Abbasî Bağdat'ında *Kitāb akriyat al-sufun* gibi sistematik hukuk-standartlarında somutlaştı.

        Sözcüğün etimolojik-akrabaları: Arap *ʿaib* (kusur, kusurlu-mal), *muʿawwar* (hasarlı), *taʿwīr* (hasar-verme, sabotaj) sözcükleri aynı *ʿ-w-r* kökünden. Bu Arap kök-ailesinin tümü ticaret-kayıp-hesabı pratiğinin kavramsal-dünyasını oluşturur; modern *average* sözcüğünün altında üç bin yıllık bir Sami ticaret-hukuku-pratiği kavramsal-yapısı yatar.
      en: |
        In pre-Islamic Arabia, the caravan-trader (*qāfila*) and the sea-trader (*tājir al-baḥr*) partners had inherited the practice of sharing voyage-loss from the oral legal-tradition. The root of *ʿawārīya* is the Semitic verb root *ʿ-w-r*, "to suffer-damage, to find-fault" — cognate with Akkadian *ḥalāqu* (destruction), Hebrew *ʿāwâ* (to be-crooked, to be-mistaken). In the annual two-seasonal caravan-organisation of the Meccan-Medinan trade caravans (Quraysh traders, sixth-seventh century), common-loss sharing was a standard-principle; the practical-economic infrastructure underlies the verse "the caravan-voyage of winter and summer" (Qurʾān 106:2).

        After the seventh-century Islamic conquest, the pre-Islamic Arab caravan-trader practice was integrated into the Mediterranean and Indian Ocean sea-trade practice. When the old-Byzantine merchant-centres of Damascus (conquest of 636) and Alexandria (conquest of 642) passed into the Arab-Islamic world, the Byzantine maritime-law tradition *Lex Rhodia* (the old-Greek Rhodes-sea-law, the Mediterranean standard from the third century BC) was synthesised with the Arab pre-Islamic *ʿawārīya* practice. This synthesis was concretised in systematic legal-standards like *Kitāb akriyat al-sufun* in eighth-ninth-century Abbasid Baghdad.

        Etymological-relatives of the word: Arabic *ʿaib* (defect, defective-goods), *muʿawwar* (damaged), *taʿwīr* (damage-causing, sabotage) come from the same *ʿ-w-r* root. All of this Arab root-family constitutes the conceptual-world of the trade-loss-accounting practice; beneath the modern word *average* lies a three-thousand-year Semitic trade-legal-practice conceptual-structure.
      ar: |

        بَعدَ الفَتحِ الإسلاميِّ، اندَمَجَت مُمارَسةُ تاجِرِ القافِلةِ العَرَبيِّ ما قَبلَ الإسلامِ في مُمارَسةِ تِجارةِ بَحرِ المُتَوَسِّطِ وَالمُحيطِ الهنديّ. وَتَحتَ كَلِمةِ *average* الحَديثةِ بِنيةٌ مَفهوميّةٌ ساميّةٌ لِمُمارَسةِ التِّجارةِ-القانونِ تَمتَدُّ ثَلاثةَ آلافِ سَنة.

      tr: |
        Akdeniz denizci-hukukunun en eski yazılı standart-kaynağı *Lex Rhodia de Iactu* (Rodos Yük-Atış-Hukuku, MS Ö 3. yy dolaylarında yazılmış) — bir gemi-batma-tehlikesinde yükün denize atılma kararının hukuki standartını koyan ilk denizci-prensip. Roma İmparatorluğu (MS Ö 1. yy itibariyle) bu Rodos-hukukunu *Digesta*'da (Justinian'ın 6. yy hukuk-kodifikasyonu, *Digest 14.2*) standartlaştırarak Doğu-Akdeniz denizci-hukuk altyapısının temeli yaptı. Bizans-Roma denizci-pratiği (7. yy'a kadar) bu *Lex Rhodia* standardını sürdürdü.

        7. yy Arap fethinin Levant ve Mısır'ı (636-642) İslam idaresine getirmesi sonrası, Arap-İslam denizci-pratiği pre-İslam Arap *ʿawārīya* prensibini Bizans-Rodos *Lex Rhodia* prensibiyle birleştirdi. Bu sentez sonucunda 9.-12. yy Akdeniz Arap-İslam dünyasının denizci-hukuk altyapısı oluştu; *ʿawārīya* sözcüğü bu sentez sürecinin teknik-adı, ama prensibin altında iki ayrı geleneğin (Arap pre-İslam + Bizans-Yunan-Rodos) birleşik mirası var.

        12.-13. yy Akdeniz denizci-pratiğinin Latin Avrupasına aktarımı, bu Arap-İslam sentezini İtalyan tüccar-konsolosluklarına aktardı. Modern Avrupa denizci-hukuku böylece tarihsel olarak üç-tabakalı: (i) antik Rodos *Lex Rhodia* (MS Ö 300-MS 600), (ii) Arap-İslam *ʿawārīya* sentezi (600-1200), (iii) Latin-Avrupa *avaria* / *general average* (1200-bugün). Modern *average* sözcüğü bu üç-tabakalı iki bin yıllık hukuk-pratiğin son sözcüksel imzasıdır; her gün dünya-deniz-ticaretinin Lloyd's-sigortasının altında, her gün üniversite-not-ortalamasının altında.
      en: |
        The oldest written standard-source of Mediterranean maritime-law is *Lex Rhodia de Iactu* (the Rhodian Law of Jettison, written around the third century BC) — the first maritime-principle to establish the legal standard of the decision to throw cargo into the sea in a ship-sinking-danger. The Roman Empire (from the first century BC) standardised this Rhodian-law in the *Digesta* (Justinian's sixth-century legal-codification, *Digest 14.2*), making it the foundation of the Eastern-Mediterranean maritime-law infrastructure. Byzantine-Roman maritime-practice (until the seventh century) carried on this *Lex Rhodia* standard.

        After the Arab conquest of the seventh century brought the Levant and Egypt (636-642) into Islamic rule, the Arab-Islamic maritime-practice combined the pre-Islamic Arab *ʿawārīya* principle with the Byzantine-Rhodian *Lex Rhodia* principle. As a result of this synthesis, the maritime-legal infrastructure of the ninth-twelfth-century Mediterranean Arab-Islamic world was formed; the word *ʿawārīya* is the technical-name of this synthesis-process, but beneath the principle is the combined inheritance of two distinct traditions (the Arab pre-Islamic + the Byzantine-Greek-Rhodian).

        The transfer of twelfth-thirteenth-century Mediterranean maritime-practice to Latin Europe carried this Arab-Islamic synthesis into the Italian merchant-consulates. Modern European maritime-law is thus historically three-layered: (i) the ancient Rhodian *Lex Rhodia* (300 BC - AD 600), (ii) the Arab-Islamic *ʿawārīya* synthesis (600-1200), (iii) the Latin-European *avaria* / *general average* (1200 to the present). The modern word *average* is the final lexical signature of this three-layered two-thousand-year legal-practice; every day beneath the Lloyd's-insurance of world-sea-trade, every day beneath the university-grade-average.
      ar: |
        أَقدَمُ مَصدَرٍ مَكتوبٍ قِياسيٍّ لِقانونِ بَحرِ المُتَوَسِّطِ هو *Lex Rhodia de Iactu* (قانونُ رَميِ الشَّحنةِ في رودِسَ، نَحوَ القَرنِ الثالثِ قَبلَ الميلاد). وَقَنَّنَتْه الإمبراطوريّةُ الرومانيّةُ في *Digesta* (تَقنينُ يوستينيانَ القانونيِّ في القَرنِ السّادس).

        بَعدَ الفَتحِ العَرَبيِّ، جَمَعَت المُمارَسةُ العَرَبيّةُ-الإسلاميّةُ بَيْنَ مَبدَأِ *العَوَارِيَةِ* العَرَبيِّ ما قَبلَ الإسلامِ وَمَبدَأِ *Lex Rhodia* البِيزَنطيِّ-الرودِسيّ. ونَتَجَ عن هذا التَّوليفِ بِنيةُ القانونِ البَحريِّ في العالَمِ المُتَوَسِّطيِّ العَرَبيِّ-الإسلاميِّ في القُرونِ من التّاسِعِ إلى الثاني عَشَر.
