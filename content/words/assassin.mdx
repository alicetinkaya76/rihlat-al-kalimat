      en: |
        From the killing of Henri IV in Paris by Ravaillac (1610), through Lincoln by Booth (1865), Sadi Carnot by Caserio (1894), to Franz Ferdinand at Sarajevo by Princip (1914), the verb *to assassinate* came to name political killing as such. The word no longer pointed to an order but to a class of crime; not to a person's wages but to the destabilisation of a state. The 1914 Sarajevo killing lit the fuse of the First World War; after it every state lodged *assassination* as a category of national-security law. A thousand years from eleventh-century Alamut, the single tie of the event to its older name was the word it used.
      ar: |

      en: |
        Between Polo's departure (1271) and his return (1295) the word *Assassini* had already been circulating in Italian and Old French chronicles, carried over from the experience of the Crusader states in the Levant. Polo's *Il Milione* told the tale of the "Old Man of the Mountain" — paradise garden, killings under hashish, the training of devoted slayers — and within a generation that story mythologised the European image of the word. Dante's *Divine Comedy* (1321) gave it "lo perfido assassin" (Inferno XIX.50), seating it in the mainstream of Italian letters. The community a Sunni chronicler had named, three centuries earlier in the Syrian mountains, as "hashish-using heretics" now lived in Europe as myth + class of crime + a single word.

        Hassan-i Sabbah (öl. 1124) 1090'da Kazvin yakınlarındaki Alamût kalesini ele geçirdi; bu, Fâtımî Halife el-Müstansır'ın 1094'teki ölümüyle doğan İsmâilî şizmin Nizârî kanadının siyasî merkezi oldu. Pers Kazvin'inden Suriye dağlarına uzanan kale ağı — Masyaf, Kadmûs, Kahf — iki yüzyıl boyunca üyelerinin düşmanlarınca *ḥaššāshiyya* ya da *mulāḥida* (sapkınlar) diye anıldığı bir dinî topluluğa ev sahipliği yaptı. Bu toplulukla 1099'dan itibaren çağdaş olan Crusader devletleri, kelimenin Avrupa kulağına doğrudan duyulduğu nokta oldu; William of Tyre, Joinville, Jacques de Vitry gibi vakanüvisler *Assassini* / *Heyssesini* gibi biçimleri Latince ve eski Fransızcaya geçirdi.       en: |
        Ḥasan-i Ṣabbāḥ (d. 1124) seized the fortress of Alamut near Qazvin in 1090; it became the political centre of the Nizari branch within the great Ismaili schism following the death of the Fatimid Caliph al-Mustanṣir in 1094. A network of fortresses reaching from Persian Qazvin into the Syrian mountains — Masyaf, Qadmus, al-Kahf — housed for the next two centuries a religious community whose members were called by their detractors *ḥaššāshiyya* or *mulāḥida* (heretics). Their being contemporary with the Crusader states (from 1099) was where the word entered European hearing directly; chroniclers such as William of Tyre, Joinville, Jacques de Vitry passed forms like *Assassini* and *Heyssesini* into Latin and Old French. The order was expelled from its fortresses and from political power between 1256 and 1273 by Mongol and Mamluk campaigns. The word stayed.
      ar: |

      tr: |
        İki tarih yan yana akar. Arapça *ḥashīsh* sözcüğü "kuru ot" anlamıyla pre-İslamî dönemden gelir; cannabis indica reçinesinin ilaç olarak adlandırılması yedinci yüzyıldan sonra kelimeyle eşleşir. İmam Câ'fer es-Sâdık'ın (öl. 765) etrafında oluşan ve İsmâ'îl b. Câ'fer'in (öl. 762) ölümüyle bir kola ayrılan grup, sonradan "İsmâ'îlîlik" diye anılacak yolun çekirdeğini kurar. Bu iki çizgi — bitkinin adı ve mezhebin adı — on birinci yüzyıla kadar birbirine değmez. *Ḥaššāshiyya* lakabını mezhep üyelerine yapıştırmak, Nizârî tarikatının düşmanlarının on birinci-on ikinci yüzyıllarda yaptığı bir leketleme işidir; modern incelemede tarikat üyelerinin haşhaş gerçekten kullandığını göstermek mümkün değildir. Lakap, kelimeyi bilen herkes için, "öteki" demenin başka bir biçimiydi.
      en: |
        Two histories run alongside one another. The word *ḥashīsh*, "dry grass," exists in Arabic from pre-Islamic times; the naming of the resin of *cannabis indica* as a medicinal substance pairs with the word from the seventh century onward. The community gathered around the Imam Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (d. 765), and the branch that split from it on the death of Ismāʿīl b. Jaʿfar (d. 762), formed the kernel of what would later be called "Ismailism." These two lines — that of the plant's name, and that of the sect's name — do not meet until the eleventh century. The fastening of the slur *ḥaššāshiyya* onto members of the sect was a defamation effected by enemies of the Nizari order in the eleventh and twelfth centuries; modern scholarship cannot show, with any confidence, that the order's members actually used hashish. The slur was, for anyone who knew the word, simply another way of saying *other*.
      ar: |
        يَجري تاريخانِ جنبًا إلى جَنب. كلمةُ *حشيش*، أي العُشبُ اليابس، موجودةٌ في العربيّةِ منذ ما قَبلَ الإسلام؛ ويَقترِنُ بالكلمةِ منذُ القرنِ السابعِ تَسميةُ رَاتنجِ قِنّبِ الهند مادّةً عِلاجيّة. أمّا الجماعةُ التي تَجمَّعتْ حولَ الإمامِ جعفرٍ الصادق (تُوُفِّيَ 765)، والفَرعُ الذي انفصلَ عنها لمّا تُوُفِّيَ إسماعيلُ بنُ جعفرٍ (تُوُفِّيَ 762)، فيَكونانِ نواةَ ما سيُسَمّى لاحقًا بـ"الإسماعيليّة." هذانِ الخَطّانِ — اسمُ النَّبتةِ، واسمُ المذهب — لا يَلتَقيانِ إلى القرنِ الحادي عَشَر. وَإلصاقُ نَعتِ *الحَشّاشيّةِ* بأعضاءِ المذهبِ كان عَملًا من أَعمالِ التَّشهيرِ قام به خُصومُ النِّزاريّةِ في القَرنَين الحاديَ عَشَر والثاني عَشَر؛ لا يَستطيعُ البحثُ الحديث أن يُثبِتَ بأيِّ ثقةٍ أنّ أعضاءَ الطريقةِ كانوا يَتعاطون الحَشيشَ فعلًا. كان النَّعتُ، لكلِّ مَن يَعرِفُ الكلمة، طريقةً أُخرى لِقَولِ *الآخَر*.
