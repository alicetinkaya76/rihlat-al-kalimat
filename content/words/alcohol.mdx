        In Semitic languages the root *k-ḥ-l* carries the sense
        "black, dark"; the name of antimony-sulphide (stibnite) powder,
        *kuḥl*, comes from this root. A cosmetic tradition reaching
        from ancient Egypt through Syria to Arabia — *kohl* applied
        around the eyelid, a fine black line. The Islamic world
        continued it. In the alchemical corpus of Jābir ibn Ḥayyān
        (eighth-ninth centuries) and al-Rāzī (854-925), the term
        *al-kuḥl* widened to cover any fine powder produced by
        sublimation — the word was technicalised. It is this technical
        sense that crossed into Toledo.
